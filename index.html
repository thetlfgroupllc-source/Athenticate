Built for Anonymity

Your Name Never
Touches Your Review.

Verify Once. Anonymous After.

One check to confirm you played pro. From there, every review you leave is detached from your name, your team, and your history.

No Backdoor for Anyone

There's no admin function that lets agents request reviewer identities. Same goes for leagues, teams, and media.

Minimum Data Stored

We only hold what's needed to confirm you played pro. Everything else gets discarded. Smaller data footprint, smaller attack surface.

Your Words, Protected

Before a review goes live, it's scanned for accidentally identifying details (a specific game, a notable injury, a one-off situation) and flagged for revision if anything could point back to you.

Straight Answers

The Questions
Players Ask First

Can my agent find out I reviewed them?
No. Your name, team, and position never appear anywhere on the platform, and there's no admin function that would let an agent request reviewer identities. What gets published is your league, your star ratings, your written review, and the tags you pick. That's the whole list. You verify once. After that, you're anonymous on every review you leave.
How do you verify I'm actually a professional athlete?
Verification runs against official league rosters and the same public records leagues and teams already maintain. Once you're confirmed as a pro, we store only what's needed to prevent duplicate accounts and discard the rest. The verification record and the review record are kept separate inside the platform. That's an architecture decision, not a policy one.
Who's behind ATHenticate?
Built by a former NFL player who saw the agent accountability gap from inside the system and got tired of watching the next generation walk into the same traps. No agent, agency, or league has equity, influence, or editorial input on this platform. Full team and advisory details will be published alongside the first sport launch.
